Brazil is a huge country, with over 200 million people. Find out about it from the English-language links below.

News and Current Affairs

  • The Folha de São Paulo newspaper has an extensive section in English.
  • The UK-based Latin American Bureau (LAB) provides progressive coverage of social and economic issues in Brazil.
  • The Guardian and The Economist both report frequently on Brazilian politics and economics.

Education

  • The State of São Paulo funds three public universities: the University of São Paulo (USP), the University of Campinas (Unicamp), and the São Paulo State University (Unesp). All three are active research.
  • The Federal Goverment funds public universities in all of Brazil's states, such as UFRJ, UFMG, UFRGS, UFF, UFPe, UFSCar, UniFeSP.
  • There are several Catholic Universities, notably PUC-RJ and PUC-SP.
